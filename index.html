Banana Cue

Banana cue is a sweet Filipino snack. The banana is fried, covered in brown sugar, and then skewered.

How to cook:

1. Heat the oil over medium low heat. Add the brown sugar and wait until the sugar starts to caramelize.

2. Place the bananas in the oil and fry them with the brown sugar so until they are covered with the sticky brown sugar and a deep golden color.

3. Drain on paper towels and serve on individual skewers.

Cheese Puto

Cheese Puto is a Filipino steamed cake. It's soft, fluffy, and cheesy!

How to cook:
1. In a mixing bowl, combine flour, sugar and baking powder.
2. Add the milk and egg and mix using a whisk or electric mixer at medium speed only until batter becomes smooth.
3. Add the water, butter and vanilla extract and mix well for a minute or two. Lastly, add the grated cheese and fold in slowly using a spatula or spoon.
4. Grease each mold with butter and fill the molds with batter up to ¾ full.
5. Add water to the bottom of the steamer and heat it gently until it reaches a low simmer. Arrange the molds in the steamer basket and steam them for 10 to 12 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.
6. Remove the lid, top each puto with cheese, and steam for 1-2 minutes until melted. Turn off the heat and let cool slightly.
7. Remove the cooked Puto Cheese from the molds and transfer to a serving plate.

Pancit Palabok

Palabok is a Filipino noodle dish with toppings and shrimp sauce, enjoyed as a comfort snack.

How to cook:

1. Fill a pot with water and bring it to a boil. Add the bihon noodles and cook until they’re firm but not too soft. Drain and set aside.

2. Heat 2 tbsp oil with achuete seeds in a pan. Once sizzling and the seeds darken, remove the seeds and keep the oil.

3. In the same pan, sauté garlic until brown. Add shrimp and flour, stirring until the mixture thickens, smells nutty, and the shrimp turn bright.

4. Pour in the water with shrimp heads, boil, and add Knorr Shrimp and Pork Cubes.

5. Remove the shrimp heads after simmering for 6-8 minutes, then season with fish sauce and pepper to make adjustments in flavour.

6. Place the Bihon noodles in a platter and pour the sauce over. Top with your choice of topping.

Buko Pie

Buko Pie is a baked treat with tender coconut meat in a creamy filling and a crisp and buttery pastry crust.

How to cook:
1. Mix flour, salt, butter, shortening, and water, then divide, roll out, and use one for the base; set aside the other.
2. Make the filling by heating a saucepan and pour-in the milk. Let boil.
3. Add the granulated white sugar and stir.
4. Put-in the young coconut meat and cook for 3 minutes.
5. Stir in cornstarch and coconut water, then cook until thickened.
6. Turn-off the heat and allow the mixture to cool down.
7. Preheat oven to 375 degrees Fahrenheit.
8. Arrange the cooked filling in the cake pan.
9. Put the second crust over the filling and seal the sides.
10. Poke holes in the top crust to prevent deformation.
11. Bake for 45–55 minutes until the crust is golden brown.
12. Let cool and serve. Share and enjoy!

Taho

Taho is a Filipino treat with silken tofu, tapioca pearls, and brown sugar syrup, served as breakfast or snack.

How to cook:

1. Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Cook the tapioca pearls according to your package instructions. This can be done in advance; just be sure to store the prepped pearls with some water or a little brown sugar syrup.

2. Combine brown sugar, water, and vanilla in a saucepan, bring to a boil, stir to dissolve, then simmer for 2-4 minutes. Remove from heat.

3. Place silken tofu in a parchment lined steamer. Steam for 10-15 minutes, or until heated through. Alternatively, you can microwave the tofu for about 2-5 minutes.

4. To serve, scoop slices of warm silken tofu into a small cup. Top with tapioca or sago pearls. Pour hot brown sugar syrup (arnibal) on top. Serve immediately, while warm.

Champorado

Champorado is a chocolate rice pudding that uses tablea. Can also be served at breakfast.

How to cook:

1. Pour water in a cooking pot. Bring to a boil.

2. Put-in the tablea and then stir. Let it dissolve in boiling water.

3. Add-in the rice. Let the water re-boil. Set the heat to low-medium and then stir almost constantly to avoid sticking. The rice should be ready when it absorbs the water (about 15 to 25 minutes).

4. Add the sugar. Stir until the sugar dissolves.

5. Transfer the champorado in individual serving bowls. Top with condensed milk.

6. Optional: Serve with tuyo.
